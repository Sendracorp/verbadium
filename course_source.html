How to use this course

This workbook takes you from zero to CEFR A1 in Central Catalan, with one explicit goal: passing the official A1 exam (the Certificat de nivell inicial de català). Every unit builds towards the four exam skills — listening, reading, writing and speaking — and the book ends with a full mock exam, model answers and examiner tips.

The rhythm of a unit

One unit = one focused session (60–90 min). Read the theory, learn the New vocabulary (every unit adds 20–25 new words and 5–8 new phrases that are never repeated, so your stock grows to 300+ by the end), work the dialogue aloud, then do all the exercises in writing before checking the answer key. Each unit ends with a Resources box: one verified listening link and one online practice link.

Pronunciation: this book uses the IPA

Every Catalan word, phrase, example and dialogue line in this book carries a transcription in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA), between slashes, e.g. llibre /ˈʎiβɾə/. The IPA is the international standard for writing sounds; learn the symbols once (next section) and you can pronounce any word in this book — and in any dictionary — without guesswork. The stress mark ˈ goes before the stressed syllable.

Accents & stress

A written accent marks the stressed syllable: grave è/ò = open /ɛ ɔ/ (cafè /kəˈfɛ/ coffee), acute é/ó = closed /e o/ (camió /kəmiˈo/ lorry). No accent? Words ending in a vowel, -s or -en are stressed on the second-to-last syllable; others on the last: hospital /uspiˈtal/ hospital, taula /ˈtawlə/ table.

Exam preparation

The Official A1 Exam

What the certificate is and who runs it

The qualification this course targets is the Certificat de nivell inicial de català (A1), aligned with the CEFR. Where you sit it depends on where you live:

In Catalonia

• The CPNL (Consorci per a la Normalització Lingüística) runs free Inicial (A1) courses for adults that lead to the certificate, all over Catalonia.
• University language services certify A1 through common interuniversity exams coordinated by CIFALC — open to the public, not only students.
→ Course & certificate routes, official portal: llengua.gencat.cat/en/serveis/aprendre_catala/a-catalunya
→ How the certificate levels work (A1 → C2): intercat.cat/en/certificates

Outside the Catalan-speaking area

The Institut Ramon Llull (IRL) runs the official exams abroad (150+ host sites, typically each May; registration opens in late winter). Be aware: the IRL ladder starts at Bàsic (A2) — there is no separate IRL A1 paper. So if you live abroad, this course is either (a) your preparation for an A1 sitting at a university CIFALC centre, or (b) the first, solid half of the road to the IRL A2 Bàsic, whose format the mock exam below mirrors at A1 difficulty.
→ What the IRL exams are: llull.cat/english/aprendre_catala/certificats_intro.cfm
→ How registration works: llull.cat/english/aprendre_catala/certificats_inscripcio.cfm

What examiners reward at A1 — and the classic English-speaker traps

Reward: communication over perfection. Full short sentences (Em dic… / Soc de… / M'agrada…); correct ser/tenir; numbers and times said confidently; the vaig + infinitive past; asking for repetition in Catalan rather than freezing (Pots repetir, si us plau? earns goodwill — silence doesn't).

Traps: ① *Tinc trenta — age needs anys: Tinc trenta anys. ② *Soc un professor — no article with jobs: Soc professor. ③ agreement — *M'agraden el cafè (singular → agrada). ④ pronouncing every letter — final infinitive -r and final -t after n/l are silent. ⑤ answering Com estàs? with *Soc bé — it's Estic bé / Molt bé. ⑥ forgetting possessive articles — la meva germana, never *meva germana.

Mock exam · A1

Full Practice Exam

Sit this in one session of about 90 minutes, after Unit 12. Exam texts carry no IPA — just like the real papers. Answers and model texts are in the answer key.

Paper 1 · Comprensió oral (listening) — 15 min

Setup (choose one): ① ask a Catalan-speaking friend (or a language-exchange partner) to read the script aloud twice; ② paste the script into any text-to-speech tool that offers Catalan; ③ self-test honestly: read the script aloud yourself at natural speed, wait an hour, then answer from memory of meaning.

Script (do not look while answering):

«Bon dia! Em dic Marta i soc de Girona, però visc a Barcelona amb el meu marit i els nostres dos fills. Treballo a l'hospital: soc metgessa. De dilluns a divendres em llevo a les sis del matí. M'agrada molt el meu barri perquè hi ha un mercat, dues farmàcies i una biblioteca. Els diumenges no treballo: esmorzo tard, llegeixo i a la tarda passegem per la platja. Ahir va ser dissabte i vam anar al cinema amb els nens.»

Task: true (V = vertader) or false (F = fals)?

  1. La Marta és de Barcelona.
  2. Té dos fills.
  3. És professora.
  4. Es lleva a les sis del matí.
  5. Al seu barri no hi ha mercat.
  6. Ahir van anar al teatre.

Paper 2 · Comprensió lectora (reading) — 30 min

Task A — Read this notice and answer.

«BIBLIOTECA MUNICIPAL — Horari: de dilluns a divendres, de 9 h a 20 h. Dissabtes, de 10 h a 14 h. Diumenges: tancat. Per fer-se el carnet, cal un document d'identitat i una fotografia. El carnet és gratuït.»

  1. Quin dia està tancada la biblioteca?
  2. A quina hora obre els dissabtes?
  3. Què cal per fer-se el carnet? (two things)
  4. Quant costa el carnet?

Task B — Match each sign (1–5) to its meaning (a–e).

  1. OBERT    (a) entrance
  2. TANCAT    (b) open
  3. ENTRADA    (c) push
  4. SORTIDA    (d) closed
  5. EMPENYEU    (e) exit

Paper 3 · Expressió escrita (writing) — 30 min

Task A — Fill in this library-card application about yourself.

  1. Nom: ___ · Cognoms: ___
  2. Data de naixement: ___ · Nacionalitat: ___
  3. Adreça: ___ · Codi postal: ___ · Ciutat: ___
  4. Telèfon: ___ · Correu electrònic: ___
  5. Data: ___ · Signatura: ___

Task B — Write a postcard (35–45 words) to a Catalan friend from your holidays. Say: where you are, what the weather is like, two things you did yesterday (past tense!), and one thing you like about the place. Open with Hola, [name]! and close with Una abraçada, + your name.

Paper 4 · Expressió oral (speaking) — 10 min

Part 1 — Answer these aloud in full sentences (the examiner's classic set):

  1. Com et dius? Com s'escriu el teu cognom?
  2. D'on ets? On vius?
  3. Quants anys tens?
  4. A què et dediques?
  5. Tens germans? Com és la teva família?
  6. Què t'agrada fer el cap de setmana?
  7. A quina hora et lleves normalment?
  8. Què vas fer ahir?

Part 2 — Role-play (the examiner plays the other part): You are at a café. Greet the waiter, order a drink and something to eat, ask how much it is, pay, thank them and say goodbye. Then, on the street, ask a stranger the way to the station and react to the answer.

Self-marking guide: give yourself one point per question for (a) a full sentence, (b) correct verb form, (c) clear pronunciation of /ə/, /ʎ/, /ʃ/. 18+ / 24 in Part 1 plus a complete role-play = comfortably at A1 standard.
