Greetings & Introductions

By the end you can: greet, introduce yourself, say where you're from, and be polite — the exact script of the oral exam's first minute.

Your name: dir-se

Em dic Anna. /əm ˈdik ˈannə/ My name is Anna. · Com et dius? /ˌkom əd ˈdiws/ What's your name? (informal) · Com es diu (vostè)? /ˌkom əz ˈðiw/ (formal) · Es diu Marc. /əz ˈðiw ˈmaɾk/ His name is Marc.

Formal vs informal. Use tu with peers; use vostè + the ell/ella verb form for respect. In the oral exam, address the examiner with vostè unless invited otherwise — it shows register awareness.

New phrases

Com estàs? — Molt bé, i tu?/kom əsˈtas — ˌmol ˈbe i ˈtu/How are you? — Very well, and you?
Molt de gust./ˌmol də ˈɣust/Nice to meet you.
D'on ets?/ˈdon ˈets/Where are you from?
Soc de Manchester./ˌsɔɡ də ˈmantʃestəɾ/I'm from Manchester.
Parlo una mica de català./ˌpaɾlu unə ˌmikə ðə kətəˈla/I speak a little Catalan.
Encara aprenc./əŋˌkaɾə əˈpɾɛŋ/I'm still learning.

Anna: Hola! Bon dia. Com et dius?

/ˈɔlə | ˌbɔn ˈdiə | ˌkom əd ˈdiws/ — Hi! Good morning. What's your name?

Tom: Em dic Tom. I tu?

/əm ˈdik ˈtɔm | i ˈtu/ — My name's Tom. And you?

Anna: Jo soc l'Anna. D'on ets?

/ˌʒɔ ˈsɔɡ ˈlannə | ˈdon ˈets/ — I'm Anna. Where are you from?

Tom: Soc de Manchester, soc anglès. I tu, ets catalana?

/ˌsɔɡ də ˈmantʃestəɾ | ˌsɔk əŋˈɡlɛs | i ˈtu | ˌedz kətəˈlanə/ — I'm from Manchester, I'm English. Are you Catalan?

Anna: Sí, soc de Girona. Molt de gust!

/ˈsi | ˌsɔɡ də ʒiˈɾonə | ˌmol də ˈɣust/ — Yes, I'm from Girona. Nice to meet you!
