Pronouns, tenir & hi ha, Numbers

By the end you can: say what you have, give your age, count to 100 and read prices — all tested in the A1 listening paper.

Tenir (to have) — and age

jotinc /ˈtiŋk/nosaltrestenim /təˈnim/
tutens /ˈtens/vosaltresteniu /təˈniw/

Quants anys tens? — Tinc trenta anys. /ˌkwanz ˈaɲs ˈtens — ˌtiŋk ˈtɾɛntə ˈaɲs/ How old are you? — I'm thirty. Also needs: Tinc gana / set / son. /ˌtiŋɡ ˈɡanə | ˈsɛt | ˈsɔn/ I'm hungry / thirsty / sleepy.

Hi ha — there is / there are

One form for both numbers: Hi ha un gat. /i ˈa uŋ ˈɡat/ There's a cat. · Hi ha tres gats. /i ˈa ˌtɾez ˈɡats/ There are three cats.

Articles

thea / some
m. sing./pl.el (l') / els /əl/ /əls/un / uns /un/ /uns/
f. sing./pl.la (l') / les /lə/ /ləs/una / unes /ˈunə/ /ˈunəs/

Before a vowel or silent h: l' — l'home /ˈlɔmə/, l'aigua /ˈlajɣwə/. Personal names take the article: en Tom, la Maria.

New phrases

Quants anys tens?/ˌkwanz ˈaɲs ˈtens/How old are you?
Tinc trenta-dos anys./ˌtiŋk ˌtɾɛntə ˈðoz ˈaɲs/I'm thirty-two.
Quin és el teu número de telèfon?/ˌkin ez əl ˌtew ˈnumeɾu ðə təˈlɛfun/What's your phone number?
Hi ha un banc a prop?/i ˈa um ˈbaŋ ə ˈpɾɔp/Is there a bank nearby?
No tinc temps./ˌno ˈtiŋ ˈtems/I don't have time.

Compounds join with hyphens: vint-i-dos /ˌbintiˈðos/, trenta-tres. Feminine: una, dues — dues taules /ˌduəs ˈtawləs/ two tables.
