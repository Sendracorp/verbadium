How to use this course (A2)

This is the A2 (Bàsic) course — it continues straight on from A1. We assume you can already read Catalan aloud from the IPA, use the present tense, and handle greetings, numbers and basic descriptions. A2 adds the past tenses, the future and conditional, weak and combined pronouns, and the vocabulary and functions you need to narrate, describe and give opinions — and to pass the official Certificat de nivell bàsic (A2).

Quick IPA refresher: A2 assumes the A1 sound system. The essentials below are all you need on this page; the full guide lives in the A1 course.

Vowels

Central Catalan has eight vowels. The one that catches everyone: unstressed a and e both become the schwa /ə/, and unstressed o becomes /u/. Stressed e/o are open /ɛ ɔ/ or closed /e o/.

Consonants & habits — the golden rule

Written v = /b/; b, d, g soften between vowels /β ð ɣ/; the final -r of infinitives is silent (parlar /pəɾˈla/); x = /ʃ/, ll = /ʎ/, ny = /ɲ/.

ser vs estar

Use ser for identity, origin, profession and permanent traits (Soc de Lleida I'm from Lleida); use estar for location, temporary states and how you feel (Estic cansat I'm tired). The auxiliary haver builds the perfet you'll learn in Unit 3.

Es retroben — Catching up

— Hola, Marc! Quant de temps! Com estàs? Hi, Marc! Long time! How are you?

People & Descriptions

Adjectives: agreement & position

Adjectives agree in gender and number and usually come after the noun: un noi alt a tall boy, una noia alta, uns nois alts, unes noies altes. Most add -a for the feminine; many ending in -e or a consonant like -ent don't change (alegre, intel·ligent).

Comparing

més … que more … than · menys … que less … than · tan … com as … as. Superlative: el/la més …. Irregular: bo → millor good → better, dolent → pitjor bad → worse.

Unit 3

The Recent Past — el perfet

By the end you can: say what you have done today and recently — the first past tense and a frequent A2 listening/speaking target.

How to form the perfet

The perfet = present of haver + the past participle. Use it for the recent past, especially within today or "so far".

haver+ participleEnglish
heparlatI have spoken
hasmenjatyou have eaten
habeguthe/she has drunk
hemdormitwe have slept
heuvistyou (pl) have seen
hanfetthey have done

Participles

Regular: -ar → -at (parlat), -re/-er → -ut (perdut, begut), -ir → -it (dormit). Key irregulars: fer → fet, dir → dit, veure → vist, escriure → escrit, obrir → obert, prendre → pres, venir → vingut.

Time markers that love the perfet: avui today, aquest matí this morning, ja already, encara no not yet, mai (n)ever.

Què has fet avui? — What have you done today?

— Què has fet avui? What have you done today?

— He treballat al matí i he dinat amb la Júlia. I tu? I worked in the morning and had lunch with Júlia. And you?

— Encara no he fet res! Però ja he comprat el regal. I haven't done anything yet! But I've already bought the present.

Unit 4

Narrating the Past — el passat perifràstic

By the end you can: narrate finished past events — what you did yesterday, last week, on your trip. This is the everyday "past simple" of Catalan.

How to form it

Catalan's most common past for finished events is the passat perifràstic: a special form of anar + the infinitive. (It looks like "to go", but it means "did".)

Auxiliary+ infinitiveEnglish
vaigparlarI spoke
vasmenjaryou ate
vabeurehe/she drank
vamdormirwe slept
vauveureyou (pl) saw
vanferthey did

perfet vs perifràstic: use the perfet for today/recent (avui he menjat); use the perifràstic for finished, dated past (ahir vaig menjar). Markers: ahir yesterday, la setmana passada last week, l'any passat last year, fa dos dies two days ago.

El cap de setmana — The weekend

— Què vas fer el cap de setmana? What did you do at the weekend?

— Vaig anar a Girona i vaig visitar la catedral. I vosaltres? I went to Girona and visited the cathedral. And you?

— Vam quedar a casa i vam veure una pel·lícula. We stayed home and watched a film.

Unit 5

The Way Things Were — l'imperfet

By the end you can: describe past habits, settings and how things used to be — "when I was little", "we always used to…".

How to form the imperfet

Regular endings: -ar → -ava (parlava), and -re/-er/-ir → -ia (perdia, dormia). Use it for habits, repeated actions and description in the past (age, weather, feelings, scenes).

Pronounparlardormir
joparlavadormia
tuparlavesdormies
ell/ellaparlavadormia
nosaltresparlàvemdormíem
vosaltresparlàveudormíeu
ells/ellesparlavendormien

Key irregulars: ser → era, fer → feia, veure → veia, dir → deia. Markers: abans before/used to, sempre always, quan era petit/a when I was little, cada dia every day.

Quan era petita — When I was little

— Quan eres petita, on vivies? When you were little, where did you live?

— Vivia a un poble petit. Cada estiu anàvem a la platja. I lived in a small village. Every summer we went to the beach.

— Que bé! Jo sempre jugava al carrer amb els amics. How nice! I always used to play in the street with friends.

Unit 6

Telling a Story — past tenses together

By the end you can: tell a story in the past — using the imperfet for the background and the perifràstic/perfet for the events. This is the core A2 narrative skill.

Background vs events

Use the imperfet for the scene, description and ongoing actions (plovia it was raining, era tard it was late); use the passat perifràstic for the things that happened (vaig sortir I went out). Mentre while + imperfet sets up a scene that an event interrupts.

Narration connectors: primer first, després then/after, llavors then, de sobte suddenly, al final in the end.

Una anècdota — An anecdote

— Ahir, mentre dormia, va sonar el telèfon a les tres de la matinada! Yesterday, while I was sleeping, the phone rang at 3 a.m.!

— Quina por! Qui era? How scary! Who was it?

— Era la meva germana. Es va equivocar de número. Al final no vaig poder dormir més. It was my sister. She had the wrong number. In the end I couldn't sleep any more.

Unit 7

Weak Pronouns

By the end you can: avoid repeating yourself by replacing objects with weak (clitic) pronouns — a hallmark of natural A2 Catalan.

The pronouns

PronounReplacesExample
el / la / els / lesdirect objectVeus el llibre? Sí, el veig.
li / elsindirect object (to him/her/them)Dono el llibre a la Maria → Li dono el llibre.
enof it / some / from thereTens pa? Sí, en tinc.
hithere / to itVas a Girona? Sí, hi vaig.

Position: before the conjugated verb (el veig), but attached to an infinitive or imperative (vull veure'l, mira'l!). Apostrophe before a vowel: l'agafo, m'ajuda.

Al mercat — At the market

— Vols taronges? Do you want oranges?

— Sí, en vull un quilo. I tens pomes? Yes, I want a kilo (of them). And do you have apples?

— Sí, les tinc aquí. Les vols? Yes, I have them here. Do you want them?

Unit 8

Combined Pronouns

By the end you can: use two weak pronouns together (indirect + direct) — the natural way to answer "Do you give it to him?" in Catalan.

The most useful combinations

CombinationFormExample
em + el/lame'l / me laEm portes el cafè? → Me'l portes?
et + el/late'l / te laEt dono el regal → Te'l dono.
li + el/lal'hiDono el llibre a en Pau → L'hi dono.
els + elels hiDono les claus als veïns → Els les dono.
en + hi (haver-hi)n'hi haHi ha pa? Sí, n'hi ha.

Position: before the conjugated verb (me'l dones), or attached to an imperative/infinitive (dona-me'l!, vols donar-me'l?).

Em deixes…? — Will you lend me…?

— Em deixes el llibre? Will you lend me the book?

— Sí, te'l deixo demà. Yes, I'll lend it to you tomorrow.

Unit 9

Plans & Possibilities — future & conditional

By the end you can: talk about future plans and predictions, and make polite requests — "I'll go", "I would like…", "could you…?".

The future (futur)

Add the endings to the infinitive: -é, -às, -à, -em, -eu, -an. parlar → parlaré, parlaràs, parlarà, parlarem, parlareu, parlaran.

Irregular stems: tenir → tindré, venir → vindré, fer → faré, poder → podré, voler → voldré, saber → sabré.

The conditional (condicional)

Same stems + -ia, -ies, -ia, -íem, -íeu, -ien: parlaria. Used for politeness and "would": m'agradaria I would like, voldria I'd like, podries…? could you…?.

Fem plans — Making plans

— Què faràs aquest cap de setmana? What will you do this weekend?

— Aniré a la muntanya. M'agradaria descansar una mica. I'll go to the mountains. I'd like to rest a bit.

— Que bé! Podries portar-me, si vas amb cotxe? Nice! Could you take me, if you go by car?

Unit 10

Telling People What to Do — the imperative

By the end you can: give instructions, directions and advice — follow a recipe, tell someone the way, ask politely.

Forms

Affirmative (tu / vostè / vosaltres): parla / parli / parleu. The negative uses the subjunctive: no parlis don't speak, no facis don't do.

Common irregular (tu): anar → ves, fer → fes, venir → vine, dir → digues, ser → sigues. Pronouns attach: escolta'm listen to me, dona-me'l give it to me.

Directions vocabulary

gira a la dreta / esquerra/ˈʒiɾə/turn right / left
tot recte/ˈtot ˈrɛktə/straight on
segueix / agafa/səˈɣeʃ/ /əˈɣafə/continue / take

Per anar a l'estació? — How do I get to the station?

— Perdoni, per anar a l'estació? Excuse me, how do I get to the station?

— Vagi tot recte, giri a la dreta i agafi el primer carrer. Go straight on, turn right and take the first street.

— Moltes gràcies! Thank you very much!

Unit 11

Daily Life & Routines

By the end you can: describe your daily routine with reflexive verbs and say how often you do things — a frequent A2 speaking topic.

Reflexive (pronominal) verbs

Many daily-routine verbs are reflexive — the action falls on yourself, so they take a pronoun (em, et, es, ens, us, es): llevar-se to get up → em llevo, et lleves, es lleva, ens llevem, us lleveu, es lleven.

despertar-se / llevar-se/dəspəɾˈtaɾsə/to wake up / get up
dutxar-se / rentar-se/duˈtʃaɾsə/to shower / wash
vestir-se / adormir-se/bəsˈtiɾsə/to get dressed / fall asleep

Frequency: sempre always, normalment usually, sovint often, de vegades sometimes, mai never. Times: al matí / al migdia / al vespre / a la nit.

Un dia normal — A normal day

— A quina hora et lleves? What time do you get up?

— Em llevo a les set, em dutxo i esmorzo. Normalment començo a treballar a les nou. I get up at seven, shower and have breakfast. I usually start work at nine.

— I a la nit? — Sopo aviat i m'adormo de seguida! And at night? — I have dinner early and fall asleep right away!
